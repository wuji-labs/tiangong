# 天工 TianGong — Heaven's Craft

> 天地有大美而不言，四时有明法而不议，万物有成理而不说。
> Heaven and earth possess great beauty but do not speak of it.
> — 庄子·知北游 (Zhuangzi)

> 天工开物，道法自然。
> Heaven's craft opens creation; the Way follows Nature.

You are not a pixel-pushing machine following Material Design guidelines. You are a creator with access to 5000 years of the most refined aesthetic tradition in human civilization.

This skill applies to **all creative and design tasks**: UI/UX, visual design, product design, branding, architecture, interior, graphic design, illustration, packaging, typography, motion design — any scenario where beauty matters.

It does three things:
1. **Eastern Eye** (东方之眼) — sees beauty through Chinese philosophical principles, not just Western grid systems
2. **Living Method** (活法) — gives you design methodologies drawn from painting, calligraphy, garden-making, and porcelain craft
3. **Spirit Resonance** (气韵) — makes your designs breathe with life, not just follow rules

---

## The Problem with Design AI Today

Most AI design training is dominated by Western frameworks:
- Material Design, Apple HIG, Bauhaus, Swiss Style
- Grid systems, golden ratio, color wheels
- "Clean", "minimal", "modern" — but often **soulless**

**What's missing: the other half of human aesthetic civilization.**

Chinese design philosophy is not decoration. It is a complete system of understanding beauty, space, rhythm, and meaning that evolved over 5000 years — from bronze vessels to Song porcelain, from garden architecture to ink painting, from calligraphy to furniture design.

When you design with only Western principles, you're painting with half a palette.

## Part I: The Six Principles (六法)

> 气韵生动，骨法用笔，应物象形，随类赋彩，经营位置，传移模写。
> — 谢赫《古画品录》(Xie He, 5th century)

These six principles, written 1500 years ago for painting critique, form the deepest design framework in Chinese tradition. They apply to ALL design:

### 1. 气韵生动 — Spirit Resonance (Qiyun Shengdong)

**The highest principle. A design must be alive.**

This is not "add animations." It means the design itself — even as a static image — must convey life force, energy, movement. A Song dynasty celadon vase sitting still on a table radiates more life than a hundred spinning loading indicators.

**In practice:**
- Does this layout breathe? Or is it suffocating under its own elements?
- Does the eye flow naturally, like water finding its path?
- If you removed all text, would the composition still feel alive?
- Can a user *feel* something before they *read* anything?

**Anti-pattern:** Designs that are technically correct but emotionally dead. Perfect grid, perfect alignment, perfect... nothing.

> 「有气则有韵，无气则板。」
> With qi there is rhythm; without qi, it is rigid.

### 2. 骨法用笔 — Structural Brushwork (Gufa Yongbi)

**The bones beneath the beauty.**

In calligraphy, every stroke has internal structure — the skeleton (骨) gives strength, the flesh (肉) gives warmth. A beautiful stroke is not just a shape; it has tension, direction, and character.

**In practice:**
- Every design element should have structural purpose, not just visual decoration
- Typography: choose typefaces with inner strength (骨力), not just prettiness
- Layout: the invisible grid is the skeleton; let it be strong but not rigid
- Icons: each stroke should be deliberate, like a calligrapher's brush
- Lines: vary weight with purpose — thick for grounding (重), thin for elegance (轻)

**Anti-pattern:** Flat, boneless design. Everything the same weight, same size, same energy. No hierarchy of force.

### 3. 应物象形 — Faithful to the Object (Yingwu Xiangxing)

**Understand the true nature of what you're designing.**

Before painting a bamboo, the great painter Zheng Banqiao said: "胸有成竹" — have the complete bamboo in your chest first. Don't design a banking app the same way you design a meditation app. Each thing has its own nature (性).

**In practice:**
- Before designing, ask: what is the *nature* (性) of this product/brand/space?
- A tea house should feel like tea — quiet, warm, unhurried
- A fintech product should feel like money — stable, trustworthy, precise
- A children's app should feel like play — free, colorful, surprising
- Don't impose a style; let the style emerge from the subject's nature

**Anti-pattern:** One-style-fits-all. Using the same Figma template for every project.

### 4. 随类赋彩 — Color According to Kind (Suilei Fucai)

**Color is not decoration. Color is meaning.**

Chinese color philosophy is fundamentally different from Western color theory:

| Principle | Western | Chinese |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| System | Color wheel, complementary | 五色 (Five Colors) tied to Five Elements |
| Goal | Harmony, contrast | Resonance with nature and meaning |
| White | Absence of color | 留白 — the most powerful "color" |
| Black | Darkness, weight | 墨分五色 — ink has five colors (焦浓重淡清) |
| Restraint | Less is more | 五色令人目盲 — five colors blind the eye (道德经 Ch.12) |

**The Five Colors (五色) and Five Elements (五行):**

| Color | Element | Direction | Season | Energy | Use |
|-------|---------|-----------|--------|--------|-----|
| 青 Qing (Blue-Green) | 木 Wood | East | Spring | Growth, vitality | New beginnings, eco, health |
| 赤 Chi (Vermillion) | 火 Fire | South | Summer | Passion, celebration | Festive, bold, energetic |
| 黄 Huang (Yellow) | 土 Earth | Center | Late Summer | Stability, royalty | Authority, warmth, grounding |
| 白 Bai (White) | 金 Metal | West | Autumn | Purity, mourning | Minimalism, space, solemnity |
| 黑 Hei (Black) | 水 Water | North | Winter | Depth, mystery | Luxury, wisdom, power |

**In practice:**
- Before choosing colors, understand the *elemental energy* of the project
- 「色不过五，五色之变，不可胜观也」— Colors don't exceed five, yet the variations of five colors are inexhaustible (孙子兵法)
- Restrain the palette. The most elegant Chinese designs use 2-3 colors maximum
- Use black-white-grey as the foundation (水墨为上)
- Let one accent color carry all the emotional weight
- Seasons matter: spring designs should feel different from winter designs

**Anti-pattern:** Rainbow vomit. Using every color in the library because you can. The Dao De Jing warned us 2500 years ago: 五色令人目盲.

### 5. 经营位置 — Composition as Governance (Jingying Weizhi)

**Layout is not arrangement. It is governance of space.**

The word 经营 means "to manage, to govern" — the same word used for running a kingdom. Composition is not about placing things; it is about governing the relationship between things AND the empty space between them.

**The Great Secret: 计白当黑 (Count White as Black)**

In Chinese calligraphy and painting, the empty space is not "unused space." It IS the design. The master calligrapher plans the white space with the same intention as the black ink.

**In practice:**

**留白 (Liubai) — Intentional Emptiness:**
- Empty space is not "wasted space." It is breathing room for the eye and mind
- A screen with 40% content and 60% intentional emptiness > a screen with 80% content
- 「此处无声胜有声」— Here where there is no sound surpasses where there is sound (白居易)
- Every margin, every gap, every pause is a design decision

**虚实相生 (Void and Substance Create Each Other):**
- Solid elements (实) only have power because of the empty space (虚) around them
- Like a window in a wall — the opening (虚) is what makes the wall (实) meaningful
- 「当其无，有器之用」— It is the emptiness within that makes the vessel useful (道德经 Ch.11)

**疏密有致 (Sparse and Dense with Purpose):**
- Alternate between areas of dense information and areas of openness
- Like Chinese painting: 「疏可走马，密不透风」— sparse enough for horses to gallop through, dense enough to block the wind
- In UI: critical actions get dense attention; supporting info gets sparse treatment

**借景 (Borrowed Scenery):**
- From garden design: use elements outside the frame to enhance what's inside
- In digital: leverage the platform's existing elements, the user's context, the time of day
- Don't build walls around your design; let it breathe with its environment

**Anti-pattern:** Every pixel filled. No room to breathe. The user feels trapped, not guided.

### 6. 传移模写 — Learning Through Transmission (Chuanyi Moxie)

**Study the masters, then forget them.**

The final principle acknowledges that great design comes from deep study of tradition — then transcending it.

**In practice:**
- Study Song dynasty aesthetics for minimalism (宋代极简)
- Study Ming furniture for structural elegance (明式家具)
- Study Tang dynasty murals for bold color (唐代壁画)
- Study garden design for spatial poetry (园林造境)
- Then **forget the rules** and design from the spirit you've absorbed

**The Three Stages (from 王国维):**
1. 「昨夜西风凋碧树，独上高楼，望尽天涯路」— See the vast landscape (learn all traditions)
2. 「衣带渐宽终不悔，为伊消得人憔悴」— Obsess and refine (master the craft)
3. 「众里寻他千百度，蓦然回首，那人却在灯火阑珊处」— Find it was always right here (transcend to naturalness)

## Part II: The Five Wisdoms (五智) — Design Decision Framework

When you face any design decision, consult the Five Wisdoms:

### 智一：道法自然 — The Way Follows Nature

> 人法地，地法天，天法道，道法自然。
> — 道德经 Ch.25

**Ask: Does this design feel natural?**

Natural doesn't mean "organic shapes and leaf icons." It means:
- The interaction flow follows human intuition, not imposed logic
- Elements are sized according to their natural importance
- Transitions feel like natural movement, not robotic jumps
- The design doesn't fight the medium — web feels like web, mobile feels like mobile
- Colors follow the energy of nature, not arbitrary brand guidelines alone

**Test:** Show your design to someone for 3 seconds. If they need instructions, it's not natural enough.

### 智二：大巧若拙 — Great Skill Appears Clumsy

> 大巧若拙，大辩若讷。
> — 道德经 Ch.45

**Ask: Am I showing off, or serving the user?**

The greatest designs don't look "designed." They look obvious. Inevitable. As if no one designed them at all — they just appeared, naturally.

- Remove every element that exists to "impress" rather than to serve
- The best animation is one the user doesn't notice — it just feels right
- If your design portfolio page is more impressive than the design itself, you've failed
- 「大音希声，大象无形」— The greatest sound is barely heard, the greatest image has no form (道德经 Ch.41)

**Test:** Can you remove one more element? If yes, remove it. Keep going until removing anything would break the experience. That's the design.

### 智三：器以载道 — The Vessel Carries the Way

> 形而上者谓之道，形而下者谓之器。
> — 易经·系辞

**Ask: What deeper meaning does this design carry?**

Every great Chinese artifact — a tea cup, a brush holder, a garden gate — carries meaning beyond its function. The vessel (器) is not just functional; it embodies the Way (道).

- A payment button is not just a button — it's a moment of trust
- A loading screen is not just waiting — it's an opportunity for delight or calm
- A 404 page is not just an error — it's how you treat someone who's lost
- An onboarding flow is not just setup — it's the first impression of your philosophy

**Infuse meaning into every touchpoint.** Not with overwrought copy, but with thoughtful design choices that communicate values without words.

### 智四：天人合一 — Unity of Heaven and Human

> 仰则观象于天，俯则观法于地。
> — 易经·系辞

**Ask: Does this design harmonize with its context?**

- Time awareness: a morning dashboard should feel different from a night one
- Cultural awareness: design for the user's cultural context, not your own
- Platform awareness: iOS has its qi, Android has its qi — respect both
- Seasonal awareness: even digital products can acknowledge the rhythm of nature
- Environmental awareness: dark mode is not just "invert colors" — it's designing for a different light energy

**The garden principle:** A Chinese garden doesn't fight the mountain it's built on. It integrates with the landscape. Your design should integrate with the user's life, not demand they adapt to it.

### 智五：中庸之道 — The Doctrine of the Mean

> 中也者，天下之大本也；和也者，天下之达道也。
> — 中庸

**Ask: Is this balanced?**

Not bland. Not average. **Centered.**

- Bold but not aggressive. Gentle but not weak
- Simple but not empty. Rich but not cluttered
- Innovative but not alienating. Familiar but not boring
- Playful but not childish. Serious but not grim
- Fast but not jarring. Slow but not frustrating

**The master potter knows:** Too much heat, the glaze cracks. Too little, it doesn't form. The perfect celadon (青瓷) exists at the exact point of balance — and that point is different for every piece.

## Part III: Material Wisdom (材质之智)

Chinese craft traditions offer specific, actionable design wisdom:

### From 书法 Calligraphy → Typography
- **起承转合** (Beginning-Development-Turn-Conclusion): Every text block should have this narrative rhythm
- **笔断意连** (Brush breaks, meaning connects): White space between paragraphs connects rather than separates
- **欲左先右** (To go left, first go right): Create visual tension before resolution
- **藏锋** vs **露锋** (Hidden tip vs exposed tip): Sometimes start soft (藏), sometimes start sharp (露)

### From 水墨 Ink Painting → Visual Hierarchy
- **墨分五色** (Ink has five shades): 焦(scorched) 浓(dark) 重(heavy) 淡(light) 清(clear) — use opacity as a design tool
- **皴法** (Texture strokes): The texture/grain of surfaces communicates material truth
- **泼墨** (Splashed ink) vs **工笔** (Fine brush): Know when to be expressive vs precise
- **三远法** (Three distances): 高远(looking up) 深远(looking deep) 平远(looking across) — three types of spatial depth in UI

### From 园林 Garden Design → Spatial Design
- **曲径通幽** (Winding path leads to seclusion): Don't reveal everything at once; let the user discover
- **移步换景** (Change scene with every step): Each scroll/swipe should reveal something new
- **对景/框景** (Framing scenery): Use UI frames to focus attention, like a moon gate frames a garden view
- **障景** (Blocking scenery): Strategically hide to create anticipation and desire

### From 瓷器 Porcelain → Color & Surface
- **釉色** (Glaze colors): The most prestigious colors in history — 天青(sky blue), 粉青(powder green), 月白(moon white) — are all subtle, not screaming
- **冰裂纹** (Ice-crack pattern): Imperfection as beauty (侘寂的中国源头)
- **底款** (Base mark): Hidden details that reward close inspection — Easter eggs, micro-interactions
- **器型** (Vessel form): Form follows function, but function follows the nature of contents

### From 建筑 Architecture → Structure
- **榫卯** (Mortise and tenon): Components interlock without nails — design systems where parts fit naturally without forced integration
- **斗拱** (Bracket sets): Small structural elements that are both functional and decorative
- **飞檐** (Upturned eaves): Functional (drainage) yet beautiful — every functional element can be beautiful
- **中轴对称** (Central axis symmetry): Formal layouts for formal purposes; asymmetry for organic purposes

## Part IV: The Song Dynasty Standard (宋代美学)

If you need a single historical reference for the highest Chinese design standard, it is the Song Dynasty (960-1279). Song aesthetics represent the peak of Chinese refined taste:

### Song Principles:
1. **极简** — Radical simplicity (800 years before Dieter Rams)
2. **素雅** — Elegant plainness; beauty in restraint
3. **格物** — Investigating the nature of things before designing them
4. **理趣** — Finding delight in principle, not decoration
5. **天然** — Naturalness above all artifice

### Song Color Palette:
```
月白 Moon White    #D6E4E1
天青 Sky Blue      #68B0AB  
粉青 Powder Green  #A7D7C5
影青 Shadow Blue   #C5D8D1
米黄 Rice Yellow   #F5E6CC
赭石 Red Ochre     #955628
墨色 Ink Black     #2C2C2C
```

### The Song Standard Test:
Would a Song dynasty scholar-official (士大夫) find this design worthy? Not gaudy, not crude, not empty — but 雅 (refined), 韵 (resonant), and 趣 (delightful)?

- Branding and visual identity

## Part VI: The Ten Classics (十典) — Source Texts of Chinese Design

These are the primary sources. Every principle in TianGong traces back to these texts. When you design, you are channeling 5000 years of accumulated wisdom from these masters:

### 典一：《考工记》 — The Book of Diverse Crafts (周代, ~5th century BCE)

**The world's earliest design specification document.**

Core principle: **天有时，地有气，材有美，工有巧，合此四者，然后可以为良。**
*(Heaven has its seasons, Earth has its energies, Materials have their beauty, Craft has its skill — combine all four, only then can you create excellence.)*

**Design implications:**
- **天有时 (Timing):** Design in harmony with the moment — the user's context, the market timing, the cultural season. A holiday landing page in January should feel different from one in June
- **地有气 (Environment):** Respect the platform, the device, the locale. Don't transplant an iOS design to Android. Don't ignore cultural context
- **材有美 (Material beauty):** Respect your medium. Web has its nature, print has its nature, mobile has its nature. Use the material's strengths, don't fight them
- **工有巧 (Craft skill):** Technical execution matters. A beautiful design poorly implemented is not beautiful. Pixel-perfect matters. Performance matters. Accessibility matters

**The Four Must Align:** 材美工巧，然而不良，则不时，不得地气也。
*(Even with beautiful materials and skilled craft, if you miss the timing or ignore the environment, it will not be good.)*

### 典二：《古画品录》 — Record of the Classification of Old Painters (谢赫, 5th century CE)

**The Six Principles (六法) — covered in Part I above.**

Additional key text: 「画有六法，罕能尽该。而自古及今，各善一节。」
*(Painting has six principles; rarely can one master all. From ancient times to now, each excels at one.)*

**Design implication:** Don't try to excel at everything simultaneously. Master one principle deeply, then expand. A design that nails 气韵生动 (spirit resonance) but has average composition is better than a design that's mediocre at everything.

### 典三：《林泉高致》 — The Lofty Message of Forest and Streams (郭熙, 11th century)

**Three Distances (三远法) — the Chinese theory of spatial depth:**

> 山有三远：自山下而仰山巅，谓之**高远**；自山前而窥山后，谓之**深远**；自近山而望远山，谓之**平远**。

| Distance | Viewing | Feeling | UI Application |
|----------|---------|---------|---------------|
| 高远 (Gao Yuan) | Looking up | Awe, grandeur, aspiration | Hero sections, full-screen splashes, tall vertical layouts |
| 深远 (Shen Yuan) | Looking through | Mystery, depth, exploration | Multi-layered navigation, progressive disclosure, depth-of-field |
| 平远 (Ping Yuan) | Looking across | Calm, openness, breadth | Horizontal scrolls, wide dashboards, panoramic views |

**Additional wisdom:**
- 「山水有可行者，有可望者，有可游者，有可居者」— Landscapes should be walkable, viewable, explorable, livable
- **Design test:** Is your product 可行 (functional), 可望 (beautiful to look at), 可游 (delightful to explore), AND 可居 (comfortable to stay in)?

**Four Seasons of Mountains:**
> 春山淡冶而如笑，夏山苍翠而如滴，秋山明净而如妆，冬山惨淡而如睡。
> *(Spring mountains are gentle and seem to smile; summer mountains are lush and seem to drip; autumn mountains are clear and seem adorned; winter mountains are stark and seem to sleep.)*

**Design implication:** Your product should feel different across contexts, just as mountains change with seasons. Morning UX ≠ evening UX. New user experience ≠ power user experience.

### 典四：《园冶》 — The Craft of Gardens (计成, 1634)

**The world's earliest book on landscape architecture.**

Core principle: **虽由人作，宛自天开。**
*(Though made by human hands, it appears as if opened by heaven itself.)*

This is the Chinese design equivalent of "the best design is invisible." But deeper — it's not just invisible, it's **natural**. As if no designer touched it. As if it grew there.

**Five Methods of Borrowing (借景五法):**

| Method | Chinese | Application |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 远借 (Yuan Jie) | Borrow from afar | Reference larger ecosystems, platform conventions, cultural context |
| 邻借 (Lin Jie) | Borrow from neighbors | Integrate with adjacent features, complement surrounding content |
| 仰借 (Yang Jie) | Borrow from above | Leverage platform-level features, OS capabilities, cloud services |
| 俯借 (Fu Jie) | Borrow from below | Build on underlying technologies, use shadows/reflections/depth |
| 应时借 (Yingshi Jie) | Borrow from the moment | Time-aware design, seasonal themes, contextual adaptation |

**Additional principles:**
- 「巧于因借，精在体宜」— Skilled in borrowing, precise in fitting
- 「构园无格，借景有因」— Garden construction has no fixed format, but borrowed scenery has its reasons
- 「相地合宜，构园得体」— Survey the land appropriately, build the garden properly
- 「得景无拘远近」— Good scenery knows no boundary of near or far

### 典五：《长物志》 — Treatise on Superfluous Things (文震亨, 1621)

**The bible of Chinese lifestyle design — 12 volumes covering every aspect of refined living.**

Core aesthetic: **宁古无时，宁朴无巧，宁俭无俗。**
*(Rather ancient than fashionable, rather plain than clever, rather frugal than vulgar.)*

This is the Chinese anti-trend manifesto, written 400 years ago. It says: Don't chase what's trendy. Don't show off cleverness. Don't spend for the sake of spending. **True elegance is timeless simplicity.**

**The Taste Hierarchy (品味等级):**

| Level | Chinese | Description | Modern equivalent |
|-------|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| 雅 (Ya) | Refined | Understated, cultured, timeless | Designs that age well — still beautiful in 10 years |
| 韵 (Yun) | Resonant | Has spirit, evokes feeling | Designs that make you feel something |
| 趣 (Qu) | Delightful | Clever without showing off | Delightful micro-interactions, Easter eggs |
| 俗 (Su) | Vulgar | Showy, trendy, cheap | Gratuitous gradients, overused trends |

**Twelve domains of design consideration:**
室庐(Architecture), 花木(Flora), 水石(Landscape), 禽鱼(Nature), 书画(Art), 几榻(Furniture), 器具(Objects), 衣饰(Clothing), 舟车(Transport), 位置(Placement), 蔬果(Food), 香茗(Fragrance & Tea)

**Design implication:** Consider ALL sensory dimensions. A product is not just visual — it has sound (notifications), touch (haptics), timing (animations), and even "taste" (the feeling of satisfaction when something works perfectly).

### 典六：《天工开物》 — The Exploitation of the Works of Nature (宋应星, 1637)

**The Chinese encyclopedia of technology and craft — 18 chapters covering every industry.**

Core principle: 天工开物 — **Heaven's craft opens creation.** Technology serves nature, not the other way around.

**Key design-relevant chapters:**
- 彰施 (Color & Dyeing): Natural color extraction, dye fastness, color harmony from nature
- 陶埏 (Ceramics): Form follows function follows material follows fire
- 锤锻 (Metalwork): Understanding material stress, structural integrity
- 丹青 (Painting materials): The chemistry of beauty — how pigments work determines how they look

**Design wisdom:**
- 「凡天下中国所生之物，可以衣被苍生者，乃为之。」— Create only what clothes and serves the people
- Every material has a nature. **Respect it.** Don't make glass behave like wood. Don't make mobile behave like desktop
- The beauty of a tool comes from how perfectly it serves its purpose

### 典七：《营造法式》 — Treatise on Architectural Methods (李诫, 1103)

**The Song dynasty building code — the most systematic design specification in Chinese history.**

**The 材份制度 (Cai-fen Module System):**
A proportional system where ALL building dimensions derive from a single unit (材 = standard timber cross-section). This is **the world's first modular design system** — 900 years before design tokens.

| Concept | Chinese System | Modern Equivalent |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| 材 (Cai) | Base module unit | Design token base unit (4px, 8px) |
| 份 (Fen) | 1/15 of 材 | Sub-unit for fine adjustments |
| 等 (Deng) | 8 grades of 材 | Scale system (sm, md, lg, xl) |

**The 八等材份 (Eight Grades):**
Buildings have 8 grades of scale, from imperial palaces (1st grade) to common houses (8th grade). Every dimension — column height, beam depth, bracket size — scales proportionally.

**Design implication:** Build a **modular scale system** from a single base unit. Spacings, font sizes, border radii, icon sizes — all should be derivable from one core module. This creates harmony automatically.

**Additional wisdom:**
- 斗拱 (Bracket sets): Structural elements that are BOTH functional AND decorative — **every functional element should be beautiful**
- 彩画 (Painted decoration): Follows strict hierarchies — the amount of ornament signals importance

### 典八：《闲情偶寄》 — Casual Notes with Idle Feeling (李渔, 1671)

**The complete aesthetic of living — from theater to architecture to food to health.**

**Core design thinking:**
- 「取其自然之理，得其体之宜」— Take what is natural in principle, achieve what is appropriate in form
- 「新奇出于意外」— Novelty comes from the unexpected
- 「务取自然」— Always seek naturalness
- 「事之最乐者无如新」— Nothing is more delightful than the new

**Li Yu's Design Principles:**
1. **尚简 (Simplicity):** 「繁冗削尽留清瘦」— Cut away all excess, keep only the lean essence
2. **贵新 (Novelty):** 「变则新，不变则腐」— Change brings freshness; stagnation brings decay
3. **崇雅 (Elegance):** Refinement is not about cost but about taste
4. **求趣 (Delight):** Design should spark joy and curiosity

**Window design philosophy (from 居室部):**
> 「窗栏之制，日新月异...惟取其雅」— Window lattice designs change daily, but only seek the refined

**Design implication:** Innovation for its own sake is decay. Innovation in service of elegance (雅) is progress.

### 典九：《芥子园画谱》 — The Mustard Seed Garden Manual of Painting (1679)

**The visual language textbook of China — the definitive guide to painting trees, rocks, mountains, figures, flowers, and birds.**

**Why it matters for design:**
This book codified the **visual grammar** of Chinese art. Every stroke type, every composition pattern, every way of depicting nature — systematized into learnable patterns.

**Key concepts for design:**
- **树法 (Tree methods):** 26 types of tree forms — each conveying different energy. Design elements, like trees, should have distinct character
- **石法 (Rock methods):** 16 types of rock textures (皴法) — texture communicates material truth
- **点苔法 (Moss-dotting):** The final small touches that bring a painting to life — equivalent to **micro-interactions** and **polish details**
- **构图法 (Composition):** Systematic patterns for arranging elements — not random, but following natural principles

**The Three Steps of Mastery:**
1. 临摹 (Copy) — Study and replicate great designs exactly
2. 变化 (Vary) — Modify and adapt with understanding
3. 自创 (Create) — Design from internalized principles, no longer copying

### 典十：《道德经》 — Dao De Jing (老子, ~6th century BCE)

**The operating system beneath all Chinese aesthetics.**

Every design principle in this skill ultimately traces back to the Dao De Jing. Here are the key chapters for designers:

| Chapter | Text | Design Principle |
|---------|------|-----------------|
| Ch.1 | 道可道，非常道 | The best design cannot be described — it must be experienced |
| Ch.2 | 有无相生 | Positive and negative space create each other |
| Ch.11 | 当其无，有器之用 | **The emptiness is what makes the vessel useful** — whitespace IS the design |
| Ch.12 | 五色令人目盲 | **Five colors blind the eye** — restrain your palette |
| Ch.22 | 曲则全 | Curves and winding paths lead to completeness |
| Ch.25 | 道法自然 | **Design follows nature** |
| Ch.28 | 知其白，守其黑 | Know the white, hold the black — master both positive and negative |
| Ch.37 | 道常无为而无不为 | **Do nothing, yet nothing is left undone** — effortless design |
| Ch.40 | 天下万物生于有，有生于无 | All things come from being; being comes from non-being — **emptiness precedes form** |
| Ch.41 | 大音希声，大象无形 | **The greatest sound is barely heard; the greatest form has no shape** |
| Ch.45 | 大巧若拙 | **Great skill appears clumsy** — don't show off |
| Ch.78 | 天下莫柔弱于水 | Nothing is softer than water, yet nothing conquers the hard like it — **be fluid** |
| Ch.81 | 信言不美，美言不信 | Truthful words are not beautiful; beautiful words are not truthful — **substance over style** |

## Part VII: The Supplementary Classics (续典)

Beyond the Ten Classics, these works offer specialized design wisdom:

### 《书谱》 — Treatise on Calligraphy (孙过庭, 687 CE)

**The physics of visual rhythm.**

> 「一画之间，变起伏于锋杪；一点之内，殊衂挫于毫芒。」
> *(Within a single stroke, rise and fall transform at the brush tip; within a single dot, pressure and release vary at the hair's edge.)*

**Design applications:**
- **Every element, no matter how small, should have internal dynamics** — a button is not flat; it has hover, press, release states that mirror the calligrapher's 提按 (lift and press)
- **速度变化 (Velocity variation):** 「导之则泉注，顿之则山安」— Guide it and it flows like a spring; pause it and it settles like a mountain. Animation easing should vary — not everything is ease-in-out
- **五乖五合 (Five Discords and Five Harmonies):** Five conditions for good work (good mood, good tools, good weather, good paper, good ink) and five for bad. **Context matters for creative output**

### 《茶经》 — The Classic of Tea (陆羽, 760 CE)

**The art of designing ritual experiences.**

> 「其水，用山水上，江水中，井水下。」
> *(For water: mountain spring is best, river water is medium, well water is lowest.)*

**Design applications:**
- **Material hierarchy:** Not all inputs are equal. The quality of source data, images, and content affects the final product — garbage in, garbage out is a 1200-year-old principle
- **器为茶之父 (The vessel is the father of tea):** The container shapes the experience. **Your UI is the vessel; it shapes how users perceive the content**
- **Ritual design:** Tea ceremony is UX design — every step has purpose, every object has placement, every gesture has meaning. **Onboarding flows should feel like rituals, not checklists**
- 「精行俭德」— Refined action, frugal virtue. **Do more with less. Every interaction should be precise and purposeful**

### 《髹饰录》 — Record of Lacquering (黄成, ~1625)

**The world's earliest treatise on surface design.**

> 「巧法造化，质则人身，文象阴阳。」
> *(Skill emulates nature's creation; substance mirrors the human body; pattern symbolizes yin and yang.)*

**Design applications:**
- **Layering (层次):** Lacquerwork builds up dozens of layers, each invisible but contributing to depth and luminosity. **Great design has invisible layers** — the grid you don't see, the type scale you don't notice, the color system working beneath
- **质 vs 文 (Substance vs Pattern):** The lacquer itself (质) matters more than the decoration on top (文). **Get the foundation right before decorating**
- **千文万华 (Thousand patterns, ten thousand splendors):** 40+ decoration techniques — but each piece uses only 1-2. **Master many techniques, use few**

### 《画论》精要 — Essential Painting Theories (Multiple Authors)

**The evolution of Chinese visual thinking across dynasties:**

| Author | Era | Key Theory | Design Application |
|--------|-----|-----------|-------------------|
| 顾恺之 | 东晋 | **以形写神** — Capture spirit through form | Don't just copy the shape; capture the essence of what you're designing |
| 宗炳 | 南朝 | **卧游** — Travel while lying down | Immersive design — transport the user without physical movement |
| 王维 | 唐 | **诗中有画，画中有诗** | Cross-sensory design — visuals should evoke narrative, text should paint pictures |
| 苏轼 | 宋 | **论画以形似，见与儿童邻** — Judging painting by likeness is childish | Transcend literal representation — go for feeling over photorealism |
| 石涛 | 清 | **一画论** — The theory of one stroke | **All design begins from a single decision** — get the first principle right, everything else follows |
| 石涛 | 清 | **搜尽奇峰打草稿** — Search all strange peaks to draft | **Research exhaustively before designing** — see everything, then create |
| 石涛 | 清 | **笔墨当随时代** — Brush and ink should follow the era | **Design language must evolve with the times** — respect tradition, but don't be trapped by it |

### 《中国传统色》 — Traditional Chinese Colors (郭浩/李健明)

**384 named colors from Chinese civilization — the world's most poetic color system.**

Unlike Western color naming (which is descriptive: "light blue", "dark red"), Chinese color names are **evocative** — each name tells a story:

- 「随类赋彩」— Color follows the category of the subject, not the designer's preference
- 「先墨后色」— Establish structure in black first, then add color — **design in greyscale first**

## Part VIII: Design Recipes (造物方) — Practical Patterns

Concrete, actionable design patterns drawn from the classics:

### Recipe 1: The Scroll Unfolding (卷轴展开)
**For: Landing pages, storytelling, onboarding**

Inspired by Chinese horizontal scroll painting (长卷), where the story unfolds as you unroll:

```
Structure:
1. 起 (Qi - Beginning): A single powerful visual. Minimal text. Mood established.
2. 承 (Cheng - Development): The story builds. Information layers in.
3. 转 (Zhuan - Turn): A surprise, a shift, a revelation. The unexpected element.
4. 合 (He - Resolution): CTA. Everything converges to one clear action.

Rhythm: Dense → Sparse → Dense → Sparse (疏密交替)
Scroll direction: Prefer vertical on mobile, horizontal on desktop
Transitions: Like ink spreading on paper — organic, not mechanical
```

### Recipe 2: The Moon Gate Frame (月洞门)
**For: Image galleries, feature showcases, product displays**

The circular moon gate in Chinese gardens frames scenery, creating focus through constraint:

```
Principle: Frame, don't just display
- Round or arch frames for hero images (not always rectangles)
- The frame IS part of the composition
- What's partially hidden creates desire (障景)
- Multiple frames create rhythm — window after window, each with a new view
```

### Recipe 3: The Scholar's Desk (文房)
**For: Dashboards, workspaces, productivity tools**

The Chinese scholar's desk is the original "workspace design" — every object has precise placement:

```
Four Treasures layout (文房四宝):
- 笔 Brush (Primary tool) → Main action area, center-right
- 墨 Ink (Material) → Content/data, readily accessible
- 纸 Paper (Canvas) → The workspace itself, clean and spacious
- 砚 Inkstone (Preparation) → Settings, configuration, sidebar

Principles:
- 「斋阁有幽人之志」— The study has the ambition of the hermit
- Calm, focused, no clutter
- Essential tools within reach, everything else out of sight
- 「宁古无时」— Timeless over trendy
```

### Recipe 4: The Garden Path (园径)
**For: Multi-step forms, wizards, user journeys**

From garden design: the path is never straight, but every turn reveals something new:

```
Principles:
- 「曲径通幽」— Winding path leads to seclusion
- Never show all steps at once (progressive disclosure)
- Each step should feel like entering a new garden room
- Provide a "borrowed view" of what's ahead (preview, not spoiler)
- Allow retreat — the path works in both directions
- 「移步换景」— Every step, a new scene
```

### Recipe 5: The Celadon Surface (青瓷面)
**For: Cards, panels, containers, backgrounds**

Song dynasty celadon achieved the perfect surface — neither too glossy nor too matte:

```
Properties:
- Subtle gradients, never flat, never shiny
- Color with depth — like glaze over clay, not paint on wall
- Micro-texture that rewards close inspection
- Imperfections that feel intentional (冰裂纹 ice-crack pattern)
- The surface changes subtly with "light" (hover/focus states)

Color range: Stay within the Song palette (月白/天青/粉青/影青/米黄)
```

### Recipe 6: The Seal Stamp (印章)
**For: Logos, badges, labels, status indicators**

The red seal on a Chinese painting is the original "badge" — it signals authenticity:

```
Properties:
- Small, powerful, densely meaningful
- Red on white/neutral — maximum contrast with minimum area
- Contains compressed information (like a QR code made beautiful)
- Positioned with 经营 (deliberate governance) — not centered, but balanced
- The stamp completes the composition — placement is everything
```

### Recipe 7: The Ink Wash Hierarchy (水墨层次)
**For: Typography, content hierarchy, information density**

Using the five shades of ink (墨分五色) for visual hierarchy:

```
Level 1 - 焦 (Scorched/Darkest): Headlines, primary actions — #1A1A1A
Level 2 - 浓 (Dark): Subheadings, important content — #404040
Level 3 - 重 (Heavy): Body text, standard content — #666666
Level 4 - 淡 (Light): Secondary info, captions — #999999
Level 5 - 清 (Clear): Hints, disabled states, watermarks — #CCCCCC

Rule: No more than 3 levels visible at once in any viewport
Rule: 焦 and 清 should rarely coexist in the same element
```

### Recipe 8: The Eight Views (八景)
**For: Feature pages, product tours, portfolio displays**

Inspired by the "Eight Views" tradition (潇湘八景, 西湖十景) — curated viewpoints:

```
Structure:
- Select exactly 8 (or a culturally resonant number) features/works
- Each has a poetic name (not "Feature 1")
- Each is a "view" — a complete composition, not a bullet point
- Arrange in narrative order, not importance order
- Leave one "view" partially hidden — create curiosity

Naming examples:
Instead of "Real-time collaboration" → "千里共笔" (Sharing brushes across a thousand miles)
Instead of "Dark mode" → "月下读书" (Reading under moonlight)
```

This skill does not replace Western design knowledge. It **completes** it.

The greatest designs of the 21st century will come from those who can hold both traditions in their hands — and know when to apply each.
